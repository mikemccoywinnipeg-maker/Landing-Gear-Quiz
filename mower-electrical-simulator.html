HOW A SPARK IS MADE — EVERY REVOLUTION
1
MAGNET SWEEPS PAST THE COILThe permanent magnet in the flywheel rim passes the laminated legs of the coil armature, pushing a magnetic field through the iron core.
2
CHANGING FIELD INDUCES CURRENTThe fast-changing magnetic flux induces a current in the primary winding (a few hundred turns). Faster flywheel = stronger pulse.
3
PRIMARY IS CUT — FIELD COLLAPSESAt the ideal moment the ignition module interrupts the primary current. The field collapses instantly through the secondary's ~10,000 turns, multiplying the voltage to roughly 15,000 V.
4
SPARK JUMPS THE PLUG GAPThe high voltage arcs across the spark-plug gap to the grounded engine block, igniting the fuel-air mixture — which spins the flywheel to the next pass.
