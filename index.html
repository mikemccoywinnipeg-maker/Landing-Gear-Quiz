Flying past the competition

Born from the necessity of moving people into roadless Nunavut.

Ookpik Aviation is locally owned and operated, drawing on more than three decades of knowledge and experience to do air transport jobs our competition simply cannot.

Unlike the western Arctic, where lakes allow float planes, the eastern Arctic above the tree line has long been reachable only by ski plane in winter or helicopter in summer. Our Super Otters changed that — opening huge new areas for exploration, tourism, search-and-rescue and government work at a fraction of the cost.

The aircraft

The DHC-3 “Super Otter”

We operate two turbine Super Otters. In each, a modern Honeywell turbine cranks out 1000 horsepower — more powerful, quieter and more fuel-efficient than the original radial. Direct-drive thrust, certified tundra tires, huge Cleveland brakes and a tail-dragger system make extraordinarily short takeoffs and landings possible.

No aircraft in the world can match its performance.

Take off in 200 feet of tundra, climb to 10,000 feet in under 5 minutes, and cruise at over 145 knots — burning only 200 litres an hour.

Super Otter vs. Twin Otter

Going where the competition cannot.

The off-strip aircraft of choice for our competition is the Twin Otter. Here's how our Super Otter compares for real eastern-Arctic work.

Ookpik DHC-3 Super Otter

  • ✓ Takes off & lands in distances too short for the Twin Otter
  • ✓ Carries more weight on strips shorter than 600 feet
  • ✓ Operates on much rougher ground
  • ✓ Hauls larger, bulkier items through an enlarged cargo door
  • ✓ Nearly 50% less fuel burn at comparable speed
  • ✓ No need for expensive drummed fuel on the return trip

Competition Twin Otter

  • × Requires longer, smoother strips to operate safely
  • × Limited payload on very short tundra strips
  • × Less tolerant of rough, irregular terrain
  • × Standard cargo door limits bulky loads
  • × Higher fuel burn for the same mission
  • × Often relies on costly drummed camp fuel
