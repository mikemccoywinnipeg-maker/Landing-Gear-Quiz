WHY "LOW TENSION"? — AND OTHER THINGS WORTH KNOWING
  • Low-tension = modest voltage, huge energy. This system delivers only ≈2,500 V to the igniter — but dumps several joules in microseconds. A magneto spark is tens of millijoules; this is hundreds of times hotter, because a turbine must relight cold, wet fuel spray at altitude. (High-tension exciters add a trigger transformer for ≈25,000 V air-gap igniters.)
  • The semiconductor pellet is the trick. A low-tension igniter isn't an open air gap — a semiconductor surface joins the centre electrode and shell. A small leakage current heats it first, ionising a path along the surface, and then the capacitor dumps everything across that path in a violent surface flash. That's why 2,500 V is enough.
  • Ignition is only needed to START the fire. Once lit, combustion is continuous — the exciter is switched off after start. It's switched on again as insurance ("continuous ignition") for takeoff, heavy rain, icing, or turbulence, and for relight after a flameout.
  • Everything is duplicated. Real engines carry two independent exciters and two igniter plugs per combustor for redundancy.
  • Respect the box. The storage capacitor can hold a lethal charge after power is removed — the bleed resistor drains it, but maintenance manuals demand a waiting period before touching leads or igniters. Watch what happens here when you cut power mid-charge.

HOW THE VOLTAGE IS BUILT UP — EVERY SPARK
1
28V IS CHOPPED INTO PULSESDC can't pass through a transformer, so the vibrator contacts open and close rapidly, switching the 28 V supply on and off through the primary winding.
2
EACH INTERRUPTION IS STEPPED UPEvery time the contacts open, the primary's magnetic field collapses through the secondary's thousands of turns — each pulse becomes a burst of ≈2,000+ volts.
3
THE RECTIFIER STACKS THE PULSESThe one-way rectifier lets each burst push more charge into the storage capacitor but never back out. Pulse by pulse, the voltage climbs — the staircase on the scope.
4
THE SPARK GAP BREAKS OVERAt ≈2,500 V the sealed spark gap ionises — a voltage-operated switch with no moving parts — and connects the loaded capacitor straight to the igniter lead.
5
SURFACE DISCHARGE AT THE IGNITERLeakage current heats the semiconductor pellet, ionising a surface path; then the capacitor dumps joules across it in microseconds — a plasma flash that lights atomised fuel even cold and soaked.
