HOW THE P-LEAD WORKS
  • Magnetos make their own electricity. A spinning magnet inside each mag induces current in a coil — exactly like the lawn-mower flywheel magneto, just engine-gear-driven. As long as the crankshaft turns, a magneto wants to fire. No battery or master switch can stop it.
  • The P-lead is the only "off switch." It runs from each magneto's primary winding to the ignition switch. To kill a mag, the switch connects its P-lead to ground, shorting the primary so no high voltage can build — the same trick as the mower kill switch.
  • The key positions ground different P-leads: OFF = both grounded • R = LEFT mag grounded (running on right) • L = RIGHT mag grounded (running on left) • BOTH = neither grounded • START = right mag grounded while the starter cranks (the left mag's impulse coupling makes the starting spark).
  • A broken P-lead = a HOT MAG. If the wire breaks, the switch can't ground that mag — it stays live even with the key OFF and out. One hand-swing of the propeller can fire a cylinder. This is why the mag check matters and why you always treat a prop as live.
