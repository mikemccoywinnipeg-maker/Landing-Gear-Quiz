HIGH-TENSION vs LOW-TENSION — WORTH KNOWING
  • Same heart, different front and back ends. Both systems store joules in a capacitor and release them through a sealed gap. This one differs at the input — AC in, so no vibrator is needed (alternating current already changes direction constantly, which is all a transformer requires) — and at the output, where a trigger transformer boosts the punch to ≈25,000 V.
  • Why 25,000 V? The high-tension igniter has a true air gap — no semiconductor pellet helping out. Cold, dense, fuel-soaked air is a stubborn insulator; it takes tens of kilovolts to rip it into a conductive plasma path.
  • The trigger transformer is the match, the capacitor is the fuel. The tiny, fast surge through its 3-turn primary induces a huge voltage in its thousands-of-turns secondary — but almost no energy. That 25 kV spike only ionises the gap. The instant the gap conducts, the storage capacitor's several joules pour through the ionised path and do the real igniting.
  • Ignition only starts the fire. As with the low-tension system: light-off, then the exciter is switched off — re-armed as continuous ignition for rain, ice, and relight.
  • Respect the box — even more. Same stored-energy hazard as the low-tension unit, plus a 25 kV output side. Bleed-down time before touching anything is mandatory.

HOW A 25,000-VOLT SPARK IS MADE
1
115V AC COMES ABOARDThe AC bus feeds the exciter. Watch the current slosh back and forth in the input wires — that constant reversal is why no vibrator is needed: AC is already "chopped" by nature.
2
POWER TRANSFORMER STEPS IT UPBecause the current is always changing, the transformer works continuously: 115 V AC in, ≈2,000 V AC out of the secondary.
3
RECTIFIER STACKS THE POSITIVE HALVESThe diode passes only the positive half of each cycle into the storage capacitor and blocks the reverse half (watch the red ✕). One charge step per AC cycle — the staircase.
4
CONTROL GAP FIRES → TRIGGER PRIMARY SURGEAt ≈2,500 V the sealed gap conducts and the capacitor slams a fast surge through the trigger transformer's 3-turn primary (white flash on the left winding).
5
25 kV SPIKE IONISES THE AIR GAP → MAIN DISCHARGEThat surge induces ≈25,000 V in the thousands-of-turns secondary (violet) — a needle of voltage that rips the igniter's air gap into plasma. The instant it conducts, the capacitor's joules pour through the secondary and the ionised gap: the fat white flash.
